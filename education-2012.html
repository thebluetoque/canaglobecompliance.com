Minute Taking Standards & Related Issues

Minute taking can be complex, tricky and challenging. Minute takers are often expected to produce clear and coherent minutes from poorly organized meetings. In this seminar we discuss principles, standards and practical tools for minute taking, and how to work effectively in a support role. The seminar is based on Eli Mina’s book: “Mina’s Guide to Minute Taking”. Topics covered will include:

  • The need for and value of minutes
  • General principles for minute taking
  • What should and what should not be recorded in minutes (content options)
  • Making minutes more readable (format suggestions)
  • Recording the decision-making process and its outcomes
  • Minutes of closed meetings (impacts of access and privacy issues)
  • Minutes approval process and its impact
  • Establishing minute taking standards across the same organization
  • Building rapport with the group and its Chairperson

101 Boardroom Problems and How to Solve Them

Boards and Councils are entrusted to govern and provide leadership to substantial organizations. Yet they often become mired in and paralyzed by dysfunction. This seminar will help your Board/Council achieve excellence in decision-making and minimize the damage from common and not so common dysfunctions. The seminar is based on Eli Mina's book "101 Boardroom Problems and How to Solve Them". Topics covered will include:

  • The challenge of achieving knowledge-based decision making
  • Typical dysfunctions on Boards & Councils and how to prevent or deal with them
  • Roles and evaluations for Board Members, the Chairperson, and the CEO
  • Duty to accept Board decisions (consensus versus majority-based decisions)
  • Duty to place organizational interests ahead of other interests (the “two-hats” dilemma)
  • Flawed interactions between the Board and Management
  • Flawed interactions between the Board and its community (citizens, members, shareholders)
  • Confidentiality: When it is justified, and the impacts of leaks from closed meetings
  • Conflict of interest guidelines

In-Camera Meeting Minutes – Webinar

What business should or must be conducted behind closed doors (“in-camera”)? Should minutes be taken in such meetings, and if so, who should take them? And, given the sensitivity of the topics discussed at such meetings, what should be captured in the minutes and what should be excluded?

In this one hour webinar we’ll discuss answers to the above questions and more. The instructor will be Mr. Eli Mina. Mr. Mina is the author of five books, including “101 Boardroom Problems and how to Solve Them” and “Mina’s Guide to Minute Taking.”
